What is the Great Vowel Shift?

The Great Vowel Shift is a series of changes affecting the pronounciation of the long vowels in English. It began in the 12th century and ended in the 18th century, but the main changes happened in the 15th to 16th centuries.

Causes

There cause of the GVS is heavily debated

Theory 1: The most accepted theory relates to the rapid migration of people after the Black Death. People moved from southeast of England to the East Midlands of England and the Central Midlands of England, which caused a clash of dialects.

Theory 2: The second theory is that many French words were adopted without modification. (loan words)

Theory 3: The third theory is that French pronounciations in the middle classes were more well respected so the shift may have unintentionally started from vowel pronounciations that inaccurately imitated French pronounciations

Discoverer

Otto Jesperson is known as the "Discoverer" of the GVS. He is a Danish linguist
